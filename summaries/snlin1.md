Igneous and sedimentary rock samples from Mars, specifically found in the Jezero Crater, were collected via the Perseverance rover. The rock samples are made up of organic carbon. The samples seem to indicate that there was past life on Mars. In these samples, results also contained nodules and reaction fronts. The sample appears to indicate that there was a chemical reaction that produced iron-phosphate and iron-sulfide. The researchers want to study the rock samples on Earth to figure out more about the mineral origin and other characteristics, which would give insight into whether there was chemical energy on Mars that could support microbial life.
