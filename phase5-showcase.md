# Phase 5: Community Testing & Reflection

**Duration:** ~20 minutes  
**Time:** 20:40-21:00  
**Goal:** Test multiple agents and share reflections on what made them effective

---

## Step 1: Open Testing (14 min)

### Explore Freely:
- Navigate between teams' repositories
- Test as many agents as you'd like
- Use YOUR summary from Phase 1 with each agent

### For Each Agent You Test:
1. Open the team's repository
2. Find their instruction file in `.github/instructions/`
3. Use their agent with GitHub Copilot Chat
4. Ask: `"Give me feedback on this summary"`
5. Read and reflect on the feedback

### Note Your Observations:
- What did you like about this agent?
- What was unique about this approach?
- How did it compare to other agents you tested?
- Would you use this agent to improve your writing?
- What specific features stood out?

---

## Step 2: Share Your Favorite (6 min)

### Vote in GitHub Discussion:

1. Navigate to the **Discussions** tab in the main hackathon repository
2. Find the discussion: **"Which agent was most effective?"**
3. Reply with:
   - **Which team's agent** you found most effective (can't vote for your own)
   - **Why it was effective** — what specific features, approach, or qualities made it stand out?

**Example Response:**
> I found **Team 3's agent** most effective because:
> - It quoted specific parts of my text and explained exactly what needed improvement
> - The tone was encouraging while being direct about issues
> - It organized feedback by rubric dimensions, making it easy to act on
> - The suggestions included concrete examples I could use

**Consider:**
- Specificity and concrete examples
- Alignment with the rubric
- Actionable guidance
- Motivating and constructive tone
- Clarity and organization

---

## Step 3: Group Debrief (3 min)

### Discussion Questions:

**What made the best agents effective?**
- Common patterns in highly-rated agents?
- Specific features that stood out?

**What surprised you?**
- Did certain approaches work better than expected?
- Any unexpected challenges?

**What did you learn about AI feedback?**
- What's easy vs. hard for AI to do well?
- What do humans still do better?
- How would you design AI feedback differently now?

**Research Insights:**
- How did feedback features (specificity, tone, etc.) impact usefulness?
- What dimensions mattered most to you?
