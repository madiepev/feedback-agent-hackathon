Research Participant Information and Consent Form

Study Title: Can AI Give Good Feedback? A Participatory Hackathon Study of Student-designed AI Feedback Agents

You are invited to participate in this research project which explores how students design and evaluate AI-powered feedback agents for science writing. Participation is entirely voluntary. Before you decide to participate, please read the following information carefully. You can ask the researcher for additional explanation if needed.

1. Research Goals

This research project explores the experiences of students when they design AI feedback agents for science writing. The purpose of this project is to understand what features of AI feedback students prioritize and how they operationalize rubric-based evaluation when co-designing feedback systems. The findings will help improve AI-mediated feedback in educational settings. The results will be presented at the ICERI2026 conference (November 9-11, 2026 in Seville, Spain) and may be published in academic journals focused on educational technology and learning sciences.

2. What We Ask of You

Participants are students attending the "Can AI Give Good Feedback?" hackathon event at Amsterdam Tech Week on June 17, 2026 (18:00-21:00) at FeedbackFruits, Amsterdam.

If you participate in the hackathon event (required for all attendees):

  • Write a 50-100 word science writing summary
  • Learn and practice using the Universal Science Writing Rubric
  • Design an AI feedback agent with your team
  • Test another team's agent and provide feedback
  • Test multiple agents and share reflections

Time commitment: 3 hours

If you consent to participate in the research (optional):

The following data artifacts created during the hackathon will be collected and analyzed:

  • Your initial writing sample (summary)
  • Your evaluation of peer summaries using the rubric
  • Agent instruction files your team creates (v1 and v2)
  • Feedback evaluations you submit via GitHub Issues
  • Your reflections on agent effectiveness shared in discussions
Important: You can participate in the hackathon event without consenting to have your data included in the research study. Research participation is completely voluntary.

3. Risks and Benefits

Risks:
There are no risks associated with participating in this research beyond those of normal educational activities. Participation involves the same activities whether or not you consent to research. If you feel uncomfortable at any point, you can withdraw from the research component without affecting your participation in the hackathon event.

Benefits:
Participation in this research will not have immediate personal benefits, but will contribute to scientific knowledge on the effective design of AI feedback systems in educational settings, particularly with regards to student perspectives on what makes feedback useful. You will gain hands-on experience designing AI-powered feedback agents and contribute to improving educational technology.

4. If You Do Not Want to Participate or Decide to Stop Your Participation

You decide if you want to participate in this research. If you decide not to participate, then you do not need to do anything or explain why you won't participate—you can still fully participate in the hackathon event. If you decide to participate in the research, then you can always withdraw your consent and stop your participation at any point in the research process. The data which has been collected up to that point will be used for the research unless you state that you would like the collected data to not be used.

5. Conclusion of the Research

Your participation stops when the hackathon event concludes (approximately 21:00 on June 17, 2026). The research project will be completed once all data has been collected and the analysis is complete, with results presented at ICERI2026 in November 2026.

6. Use and Storage of Your Personal Information

For this research, the following data will be collected from participants who consent:

  • Participant artifacts: Science writing summaries, rubric evaluations, agent instruction files (v1 and v2), feedback submissions (GitHub Issues), discussion reflections
  • Participant ID: You will be assigned an anonymous participant ID (e.g., P001, P002) that will be used to link your data

Note: No personal identifying information (name, email, etc.) is required or stored for this research. GitHub usernames used during the event will be replaced with participant IDs in all research data and publications.

The data will be stored in a secure location for ten years following publication. All publications or other dissemination of the results will ensure the confidentiality and anonymity of participants. Example quotes from agent instructions or reflections may be included in publications, but will be completely de-identified.

You can always withdraw your consent by sending an email to the primary researcher with your participant ID. If you withdraw before publication, your data will not be included in the analysis.

7. Compensation for Participation

No monetary compensation is offered for participating in this research. The winning team will receive acknowledgment during the event (optional).

8. Ethics Review and Complaints

This research design complies with the protocol of the Ethics Review Committee of the Faculty of Science (BETHCIE) at the VU Amsterdam and meets the ethical guidelines of the faculty. In case of complaints, please contact the primary researcher. If this does not resolve your complaint, you can file a complaint via bethcie.beta@vu.nl.
